What is hackX Jr?

HackX Jr is an inter-school hackathon which provides a platform for school students to transform their ideas into reality. It promotes school students to build technology-based solutions to problems they see in the society.

Participants are enabled the opportunity to develop their soft skills and find inspiration to their creativity as well as problem-solving capabilities through this competition. It paves the path for school children to be introduced to the IT pathway, create interest and eventually assess the possibilities as well as benefits of IT – based entrepreneurship. It also provides a platform for industries to address the school children, evaluate their potential and benefit from their unbiased and open-minded solutions.

Competition Details

• Students from any age group can participate in groups of 5 members and 1 mentor. (A/L students studying ICT from Physical Science stream or Technology stream are highly encouraged to take part)

• More than one team can participate from one school.

• Idea must be novel and feasible.

• Proposal and the final pitching can be done in either Sinhala or English medium.

Awareness Session

Awareness session will be conducted prior to the hackathon focusing on several aspects that might be helpful when implementing the idea and developing the proposal. Lecturers from the Department of Industrial Management, professionals and experts from the related fields will guide teams through brainstorming.

17th July

via Zoom Platform

Semi Final

Selected semi final teams can present their idea in front of a judge panel consisting lecturers and industry experts. (Semi final teams are shortlisted through proposal selection) Semi finalists will only have to pitch their idea and face a Q&A session about the product or service they are implementing and feasibility of the implementation.

21st August

via Zoom Platform

Grand Finale

The finalists are encouraged to present a final product or a reasonable prototype with the help of mentors they are assigned accordingly. (Final teams are selected strictly with the decision of judges from the Semi final) The evaluation will be done under several criteria such as feasibility, sustainability, marketing plan and adherence to time constraints.

25th September

via Zoom Platform

Competition
Specification

Students from grade 6 to after A/L (2020 A/L students) can participate with maximum 5 members for a team. (Minimum number of members for a team are one)

Every group / individual can assign a teacher-in-charge when registering for the competition but not a must.

No participant is allowed to represent two groups. Inclusion of the same member in two different teams will lead to automatic disqualification of both groups.

More than one team can participate from one school.

One team can submit more than one proposal but will select only one for the competition.

Idea must be novel and feasible.

Proposal and the pitching can be done in either Sinhala or English medium.

Application Process

Participants can get registered for the awareness session from 1st of July to 15th of July 2021 by giving details like names of team members (or as individuals), contact numbers (WhatsApp preferred) and name of the teacher-in-charge (if have a teacher).

Teams can submit their proposals from 15th of July to 31st of July 2021. Not compulsory to attend the awareness session to take part in the hackathon.

Proposals should be uploaded as a PDF document and partially completed proposals will be rejected.

Content of the proposal should be as the given template not exceeding the word limit of 3500 words. You may use diagrams, images, charts etc. in the proposal.

The proposals will be evaluated through a predefined set of criteria.
