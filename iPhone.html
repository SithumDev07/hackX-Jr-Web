What is hackX Jr?

HackX Jr is an inter-school hackathon which provides a platform for school students to transform their ideas into reality. It promotes school students to build technology-based solutions to problems they see in the society.

Participants are enabled the opportunity to develop their soft skills and find inspiration to their creativity as well as problem-solving capabilities through this competition. It paves the path for school children to be introduced to the IT pathway, create interest and eventually assess the possibilities as well as benefits of IT – based entrepreneurship. It also provides a platform for industries to address the school children, evaluate their potential and benefit from their unbiased and open-minded solutions.

Winning takes precedence over all. There’s no gray area. No almosts.

Competition Details

Students from any age group can participate in groups of 5 members and 1 mentor. (A/L students studying ICT from Physical Science stream or Technology stream are highly encouraged to take part)

More than one team can participate from one school.

Idea must be novel and feasible.

A fully functional prototype for the final demonstration is not compulsory, but encouraged.

Proposal and the final pitching can be done in either Sinhala or English medium.

Awareness Session

The awareness session will be conducted prior to the hackathon focusing on several aspects including introduction to hackathons, areas to focus on developing the solutions, technological tools that might be helpful and potential clues on how to market their product.

Lecturers from the Department of Industrial Management, professionals and experts from the related fields will guide teams through brainstorming.

22nd November 2020 from 10.00 a.m. to 12.00 p.m.

via Zoom Platform

Finale

The Final event will require the finalist teams to present their idea or product infront of a judge panel.

(Finalist teams are shortlisted through proposal selection) A prototype is preferred although not compulsory. The evaluation will be done under several criteria such as feasibility, technologies used, marketability, sustainability and adherance to time constraints.

17th December 2020 from 10.00 a.m.

via Zoom Platform
